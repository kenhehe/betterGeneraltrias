# Ask About Available Relocation Sites in Case of Demolition

If you or your family is facing demolition due to government infrastructure projects, road widening, danger zone clearing, or other public works, Tagaytay City is obligated to provide relocation assistance under Republic Act No. 7279 (Urban Development and Housing Act).

## Your Rights

Under RA 7279, no demolition may proceed without:

- Prior adequate notice (at least 30 days)
- Presence of local government officials
- Provision of relocation to qualified beneficiaries
- A court order (for structures on private land)

## Relocation Sites

Ask the City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO) or City Planning and Development Office (CPDO) about:

- Available relocation sites within or near Tagaytay City
- NHA-managed resettlement areas in Cavite
- Ongoing community-based relocation programs

## Steps to Take if Facing Demolition

1. Do not vacate immediately — know your rights first
2. Coordinate with your barangay captain
3. Visit the **CSWDO** to register as a beneficiary
4. Request written documentation of the relocation offer
5. Consult the **Public Attorney's Office (PAO)** if needed

## Requirements to Register as Beneficiary

- Valid government-issued ID
- Proof of residence (barangay certification, utility bills)
- Family composition documents (birth certificates, marriage certificate)
- Certificate of indigency (if applicable)
