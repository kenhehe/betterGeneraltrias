# Report Illegal Logging, Quarrying, or Fishing

General Trias City enforces environmental laws to protect its forests, Taal Lake, and natural resources. Residents are encouraged to report any illegal activities threatening the city's environment.

## What Can You Report?

- **Illegal logging** — unauthorized cutting of trees in protected or public areas
- **Illegal quarrying** — unauthorized extraction of sand, gravel, or other materials
- **Illegal fishing** — use of dynamite, cyanide, fine-mesh nets, or fishing in restricted zones of Taal Lake
- **Illegal dumping** — disposing of waste in waterways, forests, or unauthorized areas
- **Wildlife poaching** — hunting or capturing protected species

### Option 3 — Report to Your Barangay

Contact your barangay captain or barangay tanod to relay the report to city authorities.

## Information to Provide

When making a report, try to include:

- Location (barangay, street, landmark)
- Date and time of the incident
- Description of the activity
- Number of people involved (if safe to observe)
- Vehicle plate numbers if any (do not put yourself at risk)

## Confidentiality

Reports can be made anonymously. Your identity will be protected upon request.

## Legal Basis

Violations are prosecuted under:

- Republic Act No. 9175 (Chainsaw Act)
- Republic Act No. 7586 (NIPAS Act)
- Republic Act No. 8550 (Philippine Fisheries Code)
- Local ordinances of General Trias City
