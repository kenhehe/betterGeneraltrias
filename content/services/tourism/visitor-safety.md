## Taal Volcano Safety

General Trias City is located near **Taal Volcano**, which is one of the Philippines' most active volcanoes. Before visiting or planning a boat trip to the Taal Volcano island:

1. **Check PHIVOLCS alerts** at https://www.phivolcs.dost.gov.ph
2. Follow **Alert Level guidelines** from PHIVOLCS:
   - Alert Level 0–1: Normal/Low activity, visitors may be allowed
   - Alert Level 2–3: Elevated activity — avoid the Taal Volcano Island
   - Alert Level 4–5: Hazardous — evacuation may be ordered
3. Register with the **CDRRMO** during elevated volcanic activity
4. Know your **evacuation route**

**CDRRMO General Trias:** (046) 483-0446

## General Safety Tips for Visitors

- **Traffic:** General Trias's main highway (Aguinaldo Highway) gets congested on weekends and holidays. Plan your trip early (arrive before 8:00 AM or after 4:00 PM).
- **Weather:** The city is cool year-round. Bring a light jacket, especially in the evening. Fog and rain can reduce visibility.
- **Taal visibility:** Views of Taal are best during dry season (November–April). Cloud cover is common in June–October.
- **Food safety:** Purchase from established restaurants and markets. The **Mahogany Market** is the recommended spot for fresh bulalo and local produce.
- **Water:** Use bottled or filtered drinking water.
