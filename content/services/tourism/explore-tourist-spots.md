# Tourist Spots in General Trias City

General Trias City is one of the fastest-growing cities in the Philippines, located in the **Province of Cavite** in Region IV-A (CALABARZON). The city is rich in **revolutionary history**, vibrant festivals, world-class leisure facilities, and authentic local flavors.

---

## Historic & Religious Sites

### 1. San Francisco de Malabon Parish Church

One of the oldest churches in Cavite, originally built by Franciscan missionaries in **1611**. This national landmark was where the **Philippine national anthem was first performed** during the independence proclamation on June 12, 1898.

- **Location:** Governor Ferrer Dr., Sampalucan, General Trias City
- **Contact:** 0976 036 5475
- **Admission:** Free

### 2. Our Lady of Guadalupe Parish Church

A beloved Catholic parish serving as the spiritual center of the community. Hosts major religious celebrations, processions, and community events throughout the year.

- **Location:** Crisanto M. De los Reyes Ave., General Trias City
- **Contact:** (046) 433-0544
- **Admission:** Free

### 3. Pasong Kalabaw Historical Marker

The site of the **First Cry of Cavite** on August 31, 1896, where Filipino revolutionaries led the uprising against Spanish colonial rule — a pivotal moment in Philippine history.

- **Admission:** Free

---

## Leisure & Recreation

### 4. Eagle Ridge Golf & Country Club

A premier **36-hole championship golf course** in Barangay Javalera, regarded as one of the finest golf clubs in the Philippines. Attracts local and international golfers with its scenic fairways and world-class amenities.

- **Location:** Brgy. Javalera, General Trias City
- **Contact:** 09171220723
- **Fees:** Green fees apply; contact for rates

### 5. General Trias City Park

A central public park in the heart of the city — ideal for morning jogs, family picnics, and community gatherings.

- **Location:** General Trias City (city center)
- **Admission:** Free

### 6. City of General Trias People's Park

A well-maintained public park in Barangay Biclatan offering open spaces, walking paths, and recreational amenities for all ages.

- **Location:** Brgy. Biclatan, General Trias City
- **Admission:** Free

## Culture & Heritage

### 7. GBR Museum

A museum showcasing exhibits on local history, culture, and heritage located inside the Gateway Business Park.

- **Location:** Gateway Business Park, Brgy. Javalera, General Trias City
- **Contact:** (046) 885-7027
- **Admission:** Entrance fee applies (call ahead to confirm schedule)

### 8. Valenciana Festival (December 11–13)

An annual cultural celebration honoring the city's founding anniversary. Features the **Valenciana Cooking Festival** (all 33 barangays compete making the city's signature paella-like rice dish) and the **Valenciana Street Dance** featuring vibrant costumes and energetic school performances.

- **When:** December 11–13 annually
- **Admission:** Free to watch

### 9. Town Fiesta of St. Francis of Assisi (October 4)

The city's patron saint "Tata Kiko" is celebrated with grand parades, processions, concerts, and the unique **pabialahay** (pet blessing ceremony).

- **When:** October 4 annually
- **Admission:** Free

## Local Products & Delicacies

### 10. GenTri's Best Carabao Milk Products

The General Trias Dairy Raisers Multipurpose Cooperative produces award-winning carabao milk **yogurt, ice cream, and fresh milk** — a unique agri-tourism experience.

### 11. Eden's Pastillas

Handcrafted carabao milk pastillas (soft milk candies) — the signature **pasalubong** of General Trias.

### 12. Jail Handicrafts

Rehabilitation products from the General Trias Jail — bags, woven items (Female Dormitory), and paintings, metal crafts, and woodworks (Male Dormitory). Purchasing supports inmate rehabilitation.

## Getting to General Trias

**From Manila:**
- Via **Aguinaldo Highway** (Imus → General Trias): ~1–1.5 hours
- Via **SLEX → Carmona/Imus**: ~1–1.5 hours
- Public transport: Buses from Lawton, Cubao, or Pasay to General Trias

---

## Tips for Visitors

- **Best time to visit:** Year-round; December for the Valenciana Festival, October for the town fiesta
- **Weather:** Tropical climate; bring sun protection
- **Local specialty:** Try carabao milk products and Eden's Pastillas as pasalubong
- **History buff?** Visit during August for the First Cry of Cavite anniversary commemorations
