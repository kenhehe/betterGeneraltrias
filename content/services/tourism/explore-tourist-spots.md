# Tourist Spots in Tagaytay City

Tagaytay City is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Philippines, located in the **Province of Cavite** in Region IV-A (CALABARZON). It sits along a ridge overlooking **Taal Lake** and the famous **Taal Volcano**.

---

## Major Tourist Attractions

### 1. Taal Volcano and Taal Lake

The iconic **Taal Volcano** — one of the world's smallest active volcanoes — sits in the middle of Taal Lake and is visible from Tagaytay's ridge. Visitors can take a boat ride from **Talisay** to the lake and hike to the crater.

- **Best viewed from:** Tagaytay Ridge, Peoples Park in the Sky, Sky Ranch
- **Access:** Via Talisay town (boat rides to the volcano island)
- **Note:** Check PHIVOLCS advisories before visiting. Activity levels vary.

### 2. Peoples Park in the Sky

A hilltop park at the highest point of Tagaytay offering **360-degree panoramic views** of Taal Lake, Taal Volcano, Laguna de Bay, and Manila Bay on clear days.

- **Location:** Kay-Tikling Rd, Tagaytay City
- **Hours:** 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM
- **Admission:** Minimal entrance fee

### 3. Sky Ranch Tagaytay

An amusement park perched on the Tagaytay ridge with rides, the famous **Sky Eye** Ferris wheel, and stunning views of Taal Lake.

- **Location:** Aguinaldo Highway, Tagaytay City
- **Hours:** 10:00 AM – 9:00 PM (weekdays), 9:00 AM – 10:00 PM (weekends)

### 4. Tagaytay Picnic Grove

A sprawling outdoor park with cable car rides, picnic areas, cottages, and panoramic views of Taal.

- **Location:** Aguinaldo Highway, Tagaytay City

### 5. Puzzle Mansion

A private museum holding the world record for the largest collection of jigsaw puzzles, recognized by the **Guinness World Records**.

### 6. Tagaytay City Velodrome

A **world-class, internationally-certified velodrome** for cycling competitions and sports development. Open for training and events.

### 7. Mahogany Market

Famous for **bulalo** (beef marrow soup) and fresh produce from Batangas, this public market is a must-visit for food lovers.

## Getting to Tagaytay

**From Manila:**

- Via **Aguinaldo Highway** (Imus, Cavite → Tagaytay): ~1.5–2 hours
- Via **SLEX → Carmona/Sta. Rosa**: ~1.5–2 hours
- Public transport: Buses from Cubao or Pasay to Tagaytay (via Carmona or Imus)

**From SLEX:**

- Exit at **Sto. Tomas** or **Sta. Rosa**, then take Tagaytay-Nasugbu Highway

---

## Tips for Visitors

- **Best time to visit:** Early morning or late afternoon for cooler temperatures and clearer views
- **Weather:** Cool year-round, 12–25°C; bring a light jacket
- **Best views:** Head to the ridge along Aguinaldo Highway
- **Food:** Try bulalo, tawilis (freshwater sardine), and kesong puti (local white cheese)
