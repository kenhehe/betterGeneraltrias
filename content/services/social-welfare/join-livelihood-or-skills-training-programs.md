# Join Livelihood or Skills Training Programs

General Trias City offers livelihood and skills training programs through the City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO), the Public Employment Service Office (PESO), and in partnership with TESDA and DOLE.

## Available Programs

### TESDA Skills Training

Free short-term technical-vocational courses including:

- Food processing and culinary arts
- Housekeeping and hotel services
- Driving (NCII)
- Electrical installation and maintenance
- Dressmaking and tailoring

### DOLE Livelihood Programs

- **TUPAD (Tulong Panghanapbuhay sa Ating Disadvantaged/Displaced Workers)** — emergency employment for displaced workers
- **KABUHAYAN** — starter kits and capital assistance

### City-Level Programs (CSWDO)

- Handicraft and souvenir making (relevant to General Trias tourism)
- Pastry and baking training
- Organic farming and urban gardening
- Sewing and garment-making

### PESO Employment Assistance

The Public Employment Service Office connects job seekers with local employers and government employment programs.

## Who Can Join

- Unemployed or underemployed General Trias City residents
- Displaced workers
- Women, PWDs, senior citizens (special programs available)
- Out-of-school youth

## How to Enroll

1. Visit the **CSWDO** or **PESO** at the City Hall
2. Ask about upcoming training schedules
3. Submit your application with ID and barangay certification of residency
4. Attend the training on the scheduled dates
