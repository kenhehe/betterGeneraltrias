# Freedom of Information (FOI) Releases — Tagaytay City

Under **Executive Order No. 2 (2016)**, all executive branch agencies — including local government units — are required to adopt a Freedom of Information (FOI) policy and proactively disclose government information.

---

## What is FOI?

The **Freedom of Information** policy gives every Filipino the right to request and receive information held by the government, subject to certain exceptions (e.g., national security, personal privacy, ongoing investigations).

---

## How to File an FOI Request with Tagaytay City

### Option 1: In Person

1. Go to the **Office of the City Administrator** or the relevant department at Tagaytay City Hall
2. Fill out the **FOI Request Form** (available at the front desk)
3. Submit with a valid ID
4. Receive an acknowledgement receipt with a reference number

**Location:** Tagaytay City Hall, Akle St., Kaybagal South, Tagaytay City, Cavite
**Tel:** (046) 888-9500
**Hours:** Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM–5:00 PM

### Option 2: Online (National FOI Portal)

File your request at: https://www.foi.gov.ph

Select **Tagaytay City** as the agency and submit your request online. You will be notified of the status via email.

---

## Response Timeline

| Step                               | Timeline                                                                |
| ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Acknowledgement                    | Within **3 working days** of receipt                                    |
| Initial response (grant or denial) | Within **15 working days** (extendable by 10 days for complex requests) |
| Appeal (if denied)                 | File within **15 days** of denial                                       |

---

## Types of Information Commonly Requested

- Budget documents and financial statements
- Contracts and procurement records
- Building and business permits
- Official resolutions and ordinances
- Personnel records (subject to privacy rules)
- Environmental clearances
- Infrastructure project costs and status

---

## Exemptions

The following categories of information may be withheld:

- Information affecting national security or defense
- Ongoing criminal or administrative investigations
- Personal information protected by the Data Privacy Act
- Privileged communications (attorney-client, executive privilege)
- Preliminary drafts and internal deliberations
